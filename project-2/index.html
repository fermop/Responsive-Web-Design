Steve Jobs

The American entrepreneur, industrial designer, business magnate, media proprietor and investor.

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California, U.S.
  • 1968 - Was given a summer job by Bill Hewlett (of Hewlett-Packard) after Jobs cold-called him to ask for parts for an electronics project.
  • 1972 - Attends Reed College before withdrawing that same year.
  • 1974 - Travels through India seeking enlightenment before later studying Zen Buddhism.
  • 1976 - He and Wozniak co-founds Apple to sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer.
  • 1979 - Sees the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto, which was mouse-driven and had a graphical user interface (GUI).
  • 1985 - Was forced out of Apple after a long power struggle with the company's board and its then-CEO, John Sculley. That same year, Jobs took a few Apple employees with him to found NeXT, a computer platform development company that specialized in computers for higher-education and business markets.
  • 1986 - Helps to develop the visual effects industry when he funded the computer graphics division of George Lucas's Lucasfilm. The new company was Pixar, which produced the first 3D computer-animated feature film Toy Story (1995) and went on to become a major animation studio, producing over 25 films since.
  • 1997 - returned to Apple as CEO after the company's acquisition of NeXT. He was largely responsible for reviving Apple, which was on the verge of bankruptcy. He worked closely with English designer Jony Ive to develop a line of products that had larger cultural ramifications, beginning with the "Think different" advertising campaign and leading to the Apple Store, App Store (iOS), iMac, iPad, iPod, iPhone, iTunes, and iTunes Store.
  • 2003 - Was diagnosed with a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor.
  • 2011 - Dies of respiratory arrest (related to the tumor) at the age of 56.
  • 2022 - Was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
